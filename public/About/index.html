About

High School

I attended the prestigious Scholars Program at Saint Peter's Boys High School on a full academic scholarship in my hometown of Staten Island, New York. While there, I was a member of several volunteer clubs, the student council, and was accepted into the National Honor Society (NHS).

In Senior year I volunteered to run a tutoring program for the NHS. By year end, I expanded the program from a single elementary school, to 8 schools around the borough. I was presented with a Community Service Award for this undertaking, accompanied by a small scholarship at graduation.

Like most students at the start of Senior year, I had no idea what I wanted to pursue as a career. Thankfully, that year I happened to take a Computer Science elective in C++. At the end of the first class, I remember grinning at the words, "Hello World!" on my screen and knowing that I had found my passion.

College

For college I attended Long Island University's Honor Program (LIU) on a half academic scholarship. I majored in Computer Science, with minors in Business Administration and Information Systems Management. My graduation was one semester early and I finished in the top 10% of the class with a Bachelor of Science with Honors and a 3.7 GPA (out of 4.0).

As a Junior at LIU, I noticed the student body's lack of enthusiasm for computer science. In response, I founded the Computer Science Club with the goal of introducing basic engineering concepts through exciting and accessible means. Under my leadership as President, we held workshops on software development, networked with local internship opportunities, and participated in community service among other things. I even led a presentation on the application of VR in academics, that won the club a first place monetary prize in the LIU Student Organization Showcase.

In my time at university I led teams in various competitions and held leadership roles for multiple clubs. I became captain of the university's Data Science Team in Junior year. As a Senior, I also served as one of the 15 or so elected Representatives to the Student Government Association. In 2016, I won first place in the city-wide, Brooklyn BE.INnovative Hackathon. The following year, my concept for a used-textbook marketplace app won 3rd place (honorable mention) in the regional round of the New York State Business Plan Competition against 30 other competitors. You can learn more about these endeavors on the Projects page of this site.
